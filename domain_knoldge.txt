Here is a **short and easy explanation** of the abbreviations used in Daily Surface TAB2 Weather Data:

---

### 🌡 Temperature

* **MAX** – Highest temperature of the day (°C)
* **MIN** – Lowest temperature of the day (°C)

---

### 🌬 Wind

* **AW** – Average wind speed (km/h)
* **FFF** – Maximum wind speed during a squall (km/h)
* **D** – Wind direction code:
  1 = NE, 2 = E, 3 = SE, 4 = S,
  5 = SW, 6 = W, 7 = NW, 8 = N
* **TS** – Total number of squalls
* **TOCSQ** – Time when the strongest squall occurred
* **DU** – Duration of strongest squall (minutes)

---

### 🌧 Rain & Water

* **RF** – Total rainfall (mm) in 24 hours (ending 8:30 AM IST)
* **EVP** – Evaporation (mm) in 24 hours
* **DRNRF** – Duration of rainfall (hours & minutes)

---

### ☀ Sunshine

* **SSH** – Sunshine duration (hours)

---

### 🌩 Weather Type Codes (T)

Numbers show what weather happened:

| Code | Weather         |
| ---- | --------------- |
| 0    | Lightning       |
| 1    | Haze            |
| 2    | Mist            |
| 3    | Sand/Dust storm |
| 4    | Fog             |
| 5    | Drizzle         |
| 6    | Rain            |
| 7    | Squall          |
| 8    | Gale            |
| 9    | Thunderstorm    |
| J    | Hail storm      |
| K    | Dust fog        |
| L    | Line squall     |
| M    | Ground frost    |
| N    | Dew             |
| O    | Snow/Sleet      |

👉 **Weather Day Rule:**
0 = Did not occur
1 = Occurred

(Maximum 4 important weather types are recorded per day. Priority: Thunderstorm → Hail → Gale → Fog → Sandstorm → Squall → Rain → Drizzle)

---

### ⏰ Time Reporting

* Weather time is recorded in IST.
* If weather happens more than once, only the **first starting time** is recorded.

---

### ⏳ Duration Rule

* Maximum duration entered normally up to **804 minutes**.
* If more than 804 minutes:
  Add 800 + remaining minutes rounded to nearest 10.
  Example: 947 minutes → entered as **895** (800 + 95).

---

### 📌 Other

* **INDEX** – Station number
* **MN** – Month
* **DT** – Date

---

---

# 🎈 1️⃣ Index Data (From Weather Balloons)

## 🌡 Radiosonde Balloon

A balloon with sensors that measures:

* Temperature
* Air pressure
* Humidity

It goes high into the atmosphere and sends data back.

---

## 🎯 Pilot Balloon

A simple balloon (no sensors) used to measure:

* Wind speed
* Wind direction

Meteorologists track it using instruments.

---

# 🌩 Important Atmospheric Indexes (Easy Meaning)

These indexes tell us **how unstable the atmosphere is** and whether a storm can form.

---

## 🔥 1. SWEAT Index

**Severe Weather Threat Index**

* Measures storm risk.
* Higher value → higher chance of severe thunderstorm.

---

## 🌡 2. K Index

* Measures moisture + temperature difference.
* Higher value → more thunderstorm possibility.

---

## ⚡ 3. CAPE

**Convective Available Potential Energy**

* Measures energy available for storm formation.
* High CAPE = strong thunderstorm possible.

👉 Simple meaning:
More CAPE = more storm power.

---

## 🧱 4. CINE

**Convective Inhibition Energy**

* Measures energy that prevents storm from forming.
* High CINE = storm formation difficult.

👉 CAPE pushes storm up
👉 CINE blocks storm

---

## 🌬 5. Lifted Index

* Compares air parcel temperature with surrounding air.
* Negative value → unstable air → storm possible.

---

## 🌥 6. Showalter Index

* Similar to Lifted Index.
* Negative value → storm likely.

---

## 🌪 7. Totals Totals Index

* Combines temperature + moisture data.
* Higher value → higher instability → storm chance.

---

## 💧 Moisture & Pressure Data

* Measures humidity and air pressure at different heights.
* More moisture = more rain chance.

---

# 🌡 2️⃣ Surface Data (Ground Instruments)

This data is collected at ground level.

---

## 🌡 Temperature (Min & Max)

* Lowest and highest temperature of the day.

---

## 🌧 Rainfall

* Amount of rain in millimeters (mm).

---

## 🌬 Wind Speed

* Speed of wind in km/h.

---

## ☀ Evaporation

* Amount of water that turns into vapor.

---

## 💦 Dew Point

* Temperature at which air becomes saturated.
* High dew point = more moisture = storm friendly.

---

## 🌩 TH (Thunderstorm)

* 1 = Thunderstorm occurred
* 0 = No thunderstorm

---

## 🧊 HA (Hailstorm)

* 1 = Hail occurred
* 0 = No hail

---

# 📌 In Very Simple Words

Index data = What is happening in the sky 🌤
Surface data = What is happening on the ground 🌍

Both together help predict thunderstorms.
